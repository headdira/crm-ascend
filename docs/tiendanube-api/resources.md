- [Abandoned Checkout](/api-documentation/resources/abandoned-checkout) — The abandoned checkout is created when the customer reaches checkout's seconds step, but for some reason it does not finish the process.

- [Cart](/api-documentation/resources/cart) — The Cart resource allows the manipulation of shopping carts generated in a storefront.
- [Category Custom Fields](/api-documentation/resources/categories/custom-fields) — A custom field allows the store owner/merchant to expand their experience and control their own business through personalized and unique custom fields for categories.

- [Customer](/api-documentation/resources/customer) — A Customer of the store. Customer accounts store contact information for the customer, saving logged-in customers the trouble of having to provide it at every checkout.
- [Customer Custom Fields](/api-documentation/resources/customers/custom-fields) — A custom field allows the store owner/merchant to expand their experience and control their own business through personalized and unique custom fields for customers.
- [Discount](/api-documentation/resources/discounts) — Create, manage and apply discount rules.

- [Location](/api-documentation/resources/location) — A Location of the store where physical goods reside.
- [Metafields](/api-documentation/resources/metafields) — The metafields are Namespaced Key - Value store for Apps.
- [Order](/api-documentation/resources/order) — An order is created when a customer completes the checkout process. Orders also can be created through the API.
- [Order Custom Fields](/api-documentation/resources/orders/custom-fields) — A custom field allows the store owner/merchant to expand their experience and control their own business through personalized and unique custom fields for orders.

- [Payment Provider](/api-documentation/resources/payment-provider) — A Payment Provider, shorter name for Payments Services Provider, represents any entity which provides all the necessary resources and infrastructure for merchants and consumers to execute Transactions between them. - [Product](/api-documentation/resources/product) — A Product is an item for sale in a Tiendanube/Nuvemshop's store. It can be either a good or a service.
- [Product Image](/api-documentation/resources/product-image) — Product images could well be the single most important design aspect of any store. Without the ability to touch, hold, smell, taste or otherwise handle the products they are interested in, potential customers have only images to interact with.
- [Product Variant](/api-documentation/resources/product-variant) — Product variants allow you to group a shoe with different sizes and colors in the same product. You can only create products with up to 1000 variants.
- [Product Custom Fields](/api-documentation/resources/products/custom-fields) — A custom field allows the store owner/merchant to expand their experience and control their own business through personalized and unique custom fields for products.
- [Product Variant Custom Fields](/api-documentation/resources/products/variants/custom-fields) — A custom field allows the store owner/merchant to expand their experience and control their own business through personalized and unique custom fields for product variants.
- [Scripts](/api-documentation/resources/script) — The Script resource allows an app to register a custom Javascript file to be loaded and executed in the Merchant's storefront page. More precisely, on products (store) and payment (checkout) pages.
- [Shipping Carrier](/api-documentation/resources/shipping-carrier) — A shipping carrier (or shipping company) service provides real-time shipping rates for merchants.
- [Store](/api-documentation/resources/store) — The Store resource contains general settings and information about a Tiendanube/Nuvemshop's store.
- [Transaction](/api-documentation/resources/transaction) — Each movement of money is modeled through a Transaction object, which can be of different types (e.g. credit card, debit card, boleto, wire transfer, etc.). Each Transaction type has a Finite State Machine (FSM) that defines its current status. The Transaction Event object represents transitions in the Transaction's FSM.
